Time–bandwidth reciprocity

Change the pulse duration and observe how a rectangular pulse in time produces a sinc spectrum in frequency. The spectrum is normalised so its width is easy to compare.
Fourier transform pair
First null
Null-to-null bandwidth Bnn
Product T·Bnn (null-to-null)
Peak spectrum value AT
Transform pair
p(t) = A rect(t/T)
P(f) = AT sinc(fT)
Try: double T. The pulse becomes twice as wide, while the main lobe becomes half as wide. Here bandwidth means the main-lobe null-to-null width, so T·Bnn remains 2; other bandwidth definitions give different products.

The spectrum plot is normalised by AT. Changing A changes the true spectral magnitude, but not the displayed normalised shape.
