At an integer delay, a Nyquist pulse is zero at the other integer samples, so the echo aligns with one tap. At a fractional delay, neighbouring taps become nonzero and create intersymbol interference.

The centred mathematical pulse can produce negative-index precursor taps; a practical implementation may add a bulk delay and shift a finite approximation to causal indices. This simplified demo uses a real echo gain, corresponding to echo phase 0 or π; a general complex-baseband path gain is complex.
