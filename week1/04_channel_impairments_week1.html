Week 1: Wireless Channel Impairments

Interactive companion to the lecture slides on path loss, log-normal shadowing, AWGN, multipath propagation, and co-channel interference. Move the sliders to see how large-scale and small-scale propagation effects combine, and how the receiver moves from noise-limited to interference-limited operation.

Click the main plot to inspect the link budget at a particular transmitter--receiver distance.

Transmit power

Controls the EIRP-like link budget term in dBm.

Carrier frequency

Higher frequency gives larger free-space loss for fixed antenna gains.

Path-loss exponent n

n=2 free space; larger n represents obstructed or indoor environments.

Shadowing σ

Gaussian in dB; log-normal in linear received power.

AWGN bandwidth B

Thermal noise power increases as kTB; shown via noise floor.

Noise figure

Receiver electronics add noise beyond the ideal thermal floor.

Multipath depth

Rapid constructive/destructive fading over distances around λ/2.

Interferer power

Models co-channel interferers from frequency reuse.

Received power versus distance

Large-scale path loss is the mean trend; shadowing and multipath create measured fluctuations around it.

Lecture mapping

How the interactive elements map to Week 1 topics.

Path lossFree-space/Friis baseline and log-distance path loss model; average received power decreases with distance.
ShadowingSlow variations around the mean path-loss curve, modelled by Xσ in dB.
MultipathDelayed copies add at the receiver; constructive and destructive interference creates small-scale fading.
AWGN and SNRAdditive noise n(t), thermal noise power kTB, and SNR degradation visible in the constellation.
CCI / ACIInterferer slider demonstrates cellular reuse and the transition from SNR to SINR.
