Things to look for

  • At high SNR, received symbols cluster tightly around the ideal constellation points — few cross a decision boundary.
  • As you reduce the SNR, the clouds spread and symbols begin to land in the wrong decision region, causing errors.
  • Compare QPSK and 64-QAM at the same SNR. The noise power is identical, but 64-QAM has far less margin between neighbouring points.

Why the channel is called AWGN

Additive: the received point y equals the transmitted point x plus a noise vector n. Gaussian: the I and Q noise components are drawn from a zero-mean normal distribution, so each cloud has a symmetric, bell-shaped density. White: successive noise samples are independent and have equal power in both quadrature components.
