SNR Definition

SNR (in dB) = Received Power (dBm) − Noise Power (dBm)

SNR is a power ratio. In linear units:

SNR = Pr / Pn

When powers are expressed in dBm:

SNR(dB) = Pr(dBm) − Pn(dBm)

Example

Received Power : -80 dBm
Noise Power    : -100 dBm
SNR            : 20 dB

20 dB = (-80 dBm) − (-100 dBm)
